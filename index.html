Exercise Guide

Complete instructions for every movement in the program

Hollow Body Hold

Beginner
Core: Deep abs + hip flexors
Why: Foundation for all calisthenics skills
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Lie on back, arms overhead
  2. Press lower back flat into floor
  3. Lift shoulders and legs off ground
  4. Create "banana" shape
  5. Chin tucked, look at belly
Common mistakes: Arching back, neck strain, holding breath

L-Sit

Beginner
Core: Compression strength
Why: Leg control in space
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Sit with legs extended, hands beside hips
  2. Press hands down, straighten arms
  3. Depress shoulders away from ears
  4. Lift body off ground
  5. Keep legs parallel to floor
Progression: Bent knee ‚Üí one leg ‚Üí full L-sit

Dead Hang

Beginner
Shoulders: Decompression + mobility
Why: Builds grip and shoulder health
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Hang from pull-up bar, arms straight
  2. Relax shoulders (let them rise)
  3. Keep body still, no swinging
  4. Breathe normally
Tip: Decompresses spine after compression work

Pike Compression Lifts

Intermediate
Core: Active compression strength
Why: Bridges L-sit to advanced moves
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Sit in pike position, legs straight
  2. Hands beside hips
  3. Press down and lift heels off floor
  4. Keep legs as straight as possible
  5. Lower with control
Regression: Bend knees slightly if needed

Cossack Squats

Intermediate
Hips: Lateral mobility + strength
Why: Opens adductors, builds unilateral control
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Wide stance, toes slightly out
  2. Shift weight to one leg, bend that knee
  3. Sit deep on bent leg side
  4. Keep opposite leg straight
  5. Switch sides
Goal: Heel stays down, chest upright

Hanging Windshield Wipers

Intermediate
Core: Obliques + rotation control
Why: Builds spinal rotation strength
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Hang from bar, raise legs to L-position
  2. Keep legs together
  3. Rotate legs side to side
  4. Control the movement, no swinging
Regression: Bent knee version

Handstand (Wall)

Advanced
Total Body: Balance + shoulder strength
Why: Ultimate pushing skill
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Face wall, hands shoulder-width
  2. Walk feet up wall
  3. Bring chest toward wall
  4. Stack shoulders over hands
  5. Push through shoulders
Focus: Ribs down, glutes tight, straight line

Pistol Squats

Advanced
Legs: Unilateral strength + balance
Why: Ultimate leg control movement
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Stand on one leg
  2. Extend opposite leg forward
  3. Squat down on standing leg
  4. Keep extended leg off ground
  5. Stand back up
Progression: Box ‚Üí counterweight ‚Üí full

Dragon Flag

Advanced
Core: Extreme anti-extension strength
Why: Elite core control
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Lie on bench, grab behind head
  2. Raise entire body off bench
  3. Only shoulders and arms touch bench
  4. Lower slowly with control
  5. Keep body completely straight
Warning: Very advanced - master hollow holds first

Deep Squat Hold

Mobility
Hips: Best hip opener
Why: Full body mobility foundation
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Feet shoulder-width, toes out
  2. Squat deep, heels down
  3. Keep chest upright
  4. Push knees out over toes
  5. Shift side to side, explore range
If heels lift: Use wedge or hold support

Passive Pike Stretch

Mobility
Hamstrings: Flexibility + compression
Why: Enables L-sit and pike movements
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Sit with legs extended
  2. Fold forward from hips
  3. Relax completely, breathe deeply
  4. Let gravity do the work
  5. Hold for time (60+ seconds)
Key: Relaxation, not force. Breathe.

Hip Flexor Stretch

Mobility
Hips: Front hip opening
Why: Counteracts sitting, improves posture
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Lunge position, back knee down
  2. Tuck pelvis under (glute tight)
  3. Keep torso upright
  4. Push hips forward gently
  5. Feel stretch in front of back hip
Important: Pelvis tuck is key - don't arch back

90/90 Hip Rotations

Mobility
Hips: Internal + external rotation
Why: Improves hip health and control
‚ñ∏ How to Perform
  1. Sit with both legs at 90¬∞
  2. Front leg: shin parallel to body
  3. Back leg: shin perpendicular to body
  4. Keep chest upright
  5. Switch sides with control
Goal: Smooth transitions, no hands needed
